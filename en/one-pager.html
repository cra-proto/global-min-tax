Global minimum tax

Source of the rules

Bill C-69, which contains the Global Minimum Tax Act, received royal assent on June 20, 2024.

With the introduction of the global minimum tax in Canada, members of qualifying multinational enterprise (MNE) groups may have to register and file a return with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) as early as June 30, 2026.

Understand the global minimum tax

Background

As a members of the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) / G20 Inclusive Framework, Canada has agreed to a two-part plan to implement major reforms to the international tax system as it applies to MNEs. This plan aims to reduce the incentive for MNE groups to shift profits to low-tax jurisdictions and limit the erosion of the Canadian tax base.

The second part of this plan, called Pillar Two, introduces a 15% minimum tax that applies to large MNEs with revenues over €750 million.

Pillar Two

Pillar Two builds on the OECD/G20 Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS) project. Members of the Inclusive Framework are required to implement Pillar Two in a way that is consistent with the outcomes from the Global Anti-Base Erosion (GloBE) Model Rules, as well as the commentary and administrative guidance on the GloBE Model Rules.

The global minimum tax

The global minimum tax was proposed in Budget 2022 to implement Pillar Two. It introduces a minimum top-up tax applicable to profits of MNE groups that are within the scope of Pillar Two, if the ETR is less than 15%. It ensures that a minimum ETR of 15% applies to profits of qualifying MNE groups, in every jurisdiction in which they operate, for fiscal years that begin on or after December 31, 2023.

The Global Minimum Tax Act (the Act)

The Act implements key aspects of the Pillar Two rules in Canada based on the Global Anti-Base Erosion (GloBE) Model Rules, as well as the commentary and administrative guidance on the GloBE Model Rules. These references are approved by the Inclusive Framework and published by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development from (?) time to time.

Part 2 of the Act implements aspects of the Pillar Two Income Inclusion Rule (IIR) found in Articles 2.1 to 2.3 of the GloBE Model Rules. As a global minimum tax it imposes liability for top-up tax on certain persons in respect of a qualifying MNE group.

Part 3 of the Act allows Canada to retain taxing rights on income sourced in Canada. As a domestic minimum top-up tax it ensures any qualifying MNE groups with Canadian entities will pay the minimum rate of 15% tax in Canada in respect of those entities.

Who must register and file

A qualifying MNE group that has €750 million in revenue or more reported in the consolidated financial statements of its ultimate parent entity in at least two of the four fiscal years immediately preceding the particular fiscal year may be required to register and file with the CRA one or more of the following:

  • a GloBE information return (GIR)
  • a global minimum tax return (Part 2 or Part 3)
  • a global minimum tax notification return

One constituent entity or person may be appointed as the designated filing entity or person to file on behalf of all constituent entities or persons that must file with the CRA.

Who must file a GloBE information return

The following constituent entities of a qualifying MNE group must file a GloBE information return with the CRA:

  • the ultimate parent entity, if all the following conditions are met:
    • the ultimate parent entity is located in Canada
    • the MNE Group does not have a designated filing entity located in Canada
  • the designated filing entity, if:
    • it is located in Canada
  • each constituent entity located in Canada if the following conditions are met:
    • there is no ultimate parent entity or designated filing entity located in Canada
    • there is no ultimate parent entity or designated filing entity outside Canada that filed the GloBE information return with the CRA
    • there is no qualifying foreign filing entity
    • there is a qualifying foreign filing entity, but the CRA notifies the constituent entities that it has not received the GloBE information return from the qualifying foreign filing entity's jurisdiction

Who must file a global minimum tax return

A person that is liable to pay tax under Part 2 or Part 3 of the Act must file a Part 2 or a Part 3 global minimum tax return with the CRA.

Who must file a global minimum tax notification form

If a qualifying foreign filing entity is filing the GloBE information return for the MNE group with the tax authority of the jurisdiction where it is located, each constituent entity located in Canada must file a global minimum tax notification form with the CRA.

File a global minimum tax return

When to file

The forms must be filed by the later of June 30, 2026, or the day that is:

  • 18 months after the last day of the fiscal year, if it is the first fiscal year that an entity of the qualifying MNE groups is subject to:

    • Part 3 of the Act
    • a qualified IIR
    • a qualified UTPR

  • the day that is 15 months after the last day of the fiscal year, in any other case

A qualified IIR

A qualified IIR, for a fiscal year, means an IIR that has qualified status (including transitional qualified status) for the fiscal year as determined by the Inclusive Framework and published on the website of the OECD. (RDIR (not defined on page) admissible)

A qualified UTPR

A qualified UTPR, for a fiscal year, means a UTPR (not defined on page - Undertaxed Payments Rule?) that has qualified status (including transitional qualified status) for the fiscal year as determined by the Inclusive Framework and published on the website of the OECD. (RPII (not defined on page) admissible)
