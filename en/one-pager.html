Children's Special Allowances (CSA)

The children's special allowances (CSA) program provides payments to government departments, provincial and territorial agencies and other organizations, generally referred to as an “agency”, or individuals that care for children.

The CSA replaces the Canada child benefit when a child is in the care of an agency.

The CSA is a tax-free monthly payment for a child who:

  • is under the age of 18;
  • physically resides in Canada; and
  • is maintained by an agency.

CSA payments are to be used exclusively for the care and maintenance of the child to whom they apply. This means the child is dependent on the agency for food, clothing, shelter, medical assistance, education, training and advancement to a greater extent than on any other agency or person.

The Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) administers this program, which is governed by Children's Special Allowances Act and the Children's Special Allowance Regulations.
